Detailed Description

Struct for storing parameters for generating a desired acceleration objective \( \dot{v}_d \) for the QP inverse dynamics controller.

A way to generate \( \dot{v}_d \) is through a simple PD law around a nominal setpoint \( (q^*, v^*, \dot{v}^*) \) as:

\[ \dot{v}_d = kp (q^* - q) + kd (v^* - v) + \dot{v}^*. \]

This setpoint is typically generated by some motion planner or simple heuristics. \( \dot{v}_d \) is then used in the quadratic cost function in the QP inverse dynamics controller. The cost function typically looks like:

\[ min_{\dot{v}} (\dot{v}_d - \dot{v})^T W (\dot{v}_d - \dot{v}), \]

where \( W \) is a diagonal weighting matrix. \( W \)'s diagonal terms are from weight. Depending on the application, \( \dot{v}_d \) can also be ignored or treated as an equality constraint in the optimization. If weight(i) is positive, \( \dot{v}_d(i) \) is used as a cost term. If weight(i) is negative, \( \dot{v}_d(i) \) is used as an equality constraint. If weight(i) is zero, \( \dot{v}_d(i) \) is ignored.

This struct holds name, kp, kd, and weight.
